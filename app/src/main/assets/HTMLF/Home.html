HISTORY OF BRIGADE

Brief History of Boys' Brigade

The boys' brigade was founded by Sir Alexander Williams Smith. He was born in Glasgow, Scotland in October 27th, 1854. William A. Smith was an Army Officer before the lord called him for his work; he abandoned his military service and became a Sunday school Teacher at Free Church Mission, Northwood side hall in Glasgow Scotland. It was founded during the time parent left their children uncared for. Therefore children became wild, undisciplined and were not properly taught the Bible, hence as an enthusiastic Sunday school Teacher, he discovered what was going on and applied his military knowledge forming what is called BOYS BRIGADE in order to see that children hears the word of God. Though this Boys Brigade organisation started with many boys but due to some of the disobedience which many of our companies are facing today made some of the boys run away due to the hard (harsh) training in Brigade. so they remain only 31 boys including Sir William A. Smith and these were the people that formed the Brigade.

Brief History of Girls' Brigade

The Girls' Brigade was founded by Sunday school union in the United Kingdom in the year 1893. The founder of the the Girls' Brigade is the Late Mrs. Margaret Beauty. Girls' Brigade was shared into three branches: i.e. Girls' Brigade which was started in the 1883, Girls' Guildry which started in 1900 and the Girls' Life Brigade which started in 1902.
