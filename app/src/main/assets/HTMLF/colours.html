From the earliest times, Emblems and Colours have been used as a distinguishing mark of every Organisation.

NB: Flags which have been consecrated are called “COLOURS”

WHY DO WE PRESENT COLOURS?

  • To give reverence to Jesus Christ as our Saviour and Lord, and to the Nation and the Church.
  • To identify the presence of the Company.
  • To bring Spirit of Unity and Team Work.
  • To show the symbol of all the ideas for which the Brigades stands.

NOTES ON COLOUR PRESENTATION

Beginning of the Service.

  • It is usual for the Colours to be brought into the church immediately after the entry of the Minister and before the Service proceeds.

  • The Congregation remains standing as the Colours are carried in slow time, at the carry to the front.

  • The Colour party moves in a single file with the National Colour leading to the Chancel.

  • It is appropriate for the Minister (Agent) to receive the Colours from the bearers, taking the Company Colour first, followed by the Methodist Colour and then the National Colour last, and to place them in some prominent position where they remain during the service.

Colours position on the Chancel

  • The National Colour is placed in a position right of the Chancel (on the left from the Congregation) followed by the Methodist Colour and then the Company Colours.

  • The Colour party should then move to seats reserved for them.

Ending of Service

  • During or Singing of the last hymn, the Colour party will move to the front to receive the Colours. (The National Colour bearer leading).

  • It is appropriate for the Minister (Agent) to return the Colours to the bearers, returning the National Colour first, followed by the Methodist Colour and then the Company Colour last.

  • When all Colours have been received, custom demands that the Chancel is faced for the Benediction hence Colours will be brought to order for the Benediction.

  • The Colour party will turn about to face the Congregation.

    • NB: The Colour Party remain Attention during National Anthem or Halleluiah Chorus.

  • The Congregation will remain standing which the Colour party move in slow time from the church.

  • The Colour Party will return to the Vestry or other suitable place to handover the Colours.
